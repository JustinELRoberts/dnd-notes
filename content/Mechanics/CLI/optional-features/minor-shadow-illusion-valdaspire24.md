# Minor Shadow Illusion

_**Prerequisites**_ [Minor Illusion](/Mechanics/CLI/spells/minor-illusion-xphb.md) cantrip

When you create the image of an object in an unoccupied space using the [Minor Illusion](/Mechanics/CLI/spells/minor-illusion-xphb.md) cantrip, you can fill it with fibers of shadowstuff, causing it to become partially real. No matter what form the semi-real object takes, it still must be no larger than a 5-foot [Cube](/Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/cube-area-of-effect-xphb.md). It has AC 10 and 5 [Hit Points](/Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/hit-points-xphb.md), and it weighs 5 pounds. You can only have one semi-real illusion at a time. While this semi-real object exists, the cantrip requires [Concentration](/Mechanics/CLI/conditions.md#Concentration).

The illusion can't replicate a creature, but it can deal damage to a creature within its 5-foot [Cube](/Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/cube-area-of-effect-xphb.md) if the illusion is of an object that can deal damage. A creature that enters the illusion's area or begins its turn there must make an Intelligence saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failed save, the creature takes `1d6` damage of a type appropriate to the illusion. This damage can't trigger [Warmage Edge](/Mechanics/CLI/classes/warmage-valdaspire24.md#Warmage%20Edge%20\(Level%201\)) or any Warmage tricks. This damage increases by `1d6` when you reach Warmage level 5 (`2d6`), 11 (`3d6`), and 17 (`4d6`).
