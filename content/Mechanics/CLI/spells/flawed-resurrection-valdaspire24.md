# Flawed Resurrection
*5th-level, Necromancy*  


- **Casting time:** 1 hour
- **Range:** Touch
- **Components:** V, S, M (a drop of blood, a piece of flesh, and a pinch of bone dust)
- **Duration:** Instantaneous

You touch a dead Humanoid that has dead been no longer than 10 days and revive it as an intelligent Undead creature. The creature returns to life with 1 [Hit Point](Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/hit-points-xphb.md). This spell closes all mortal wounds, but it doesn't restore missing body parts.

The creature's type becomes Undead and it gains [Immunity](Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/immunity-xphb.md) to Poison damage and the [Poisoned](Mechanics/CLI/rules/conditions.md#Poisoned) condition. It doesn't need to eat, drink, sleep, or breathe, though it must still rest for 8 hours to gain the benefits of a [Long Rest](Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/long-rest-xphb.md).

The Undead body deteriorates over time. Whenever the creature finishes a [Long Rest](Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/long-rest-xphb.md), its [Hit Point](Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/hit-points-xphb.md) maximum permanently decreases by 1. The creature's [Hit Point](Mechanics/CLI/rules/variant-rules/hit-points-xphb.md) maximum can only be restored by the [Greater Restoration](Mechanics/CLI/spells/greater-restoration-xphb.md) spell. If the creature dies and is later targeted by [Resurrection](Mechanics/CLI/spells/resurrection-xphb.md) or similar magic, it can be returned to life with its original creature type.

**Classes**: [Necromancer](Mechanics/CLI/lists/list-spells-classes-necromancer.md)
