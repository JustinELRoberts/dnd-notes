## Summary

Seeking refuge at the Starlight Inn after their arena triumph, the party encountered the silent [[genasi-water-mpmm|Genasi (Water)]] [[Aven Tide]], whose stolen voice led [[Callie Dawnsong|Cal]] into a hag-tainted memory revealing a mysterious [[Crooked Staff|crooked staff]] artifact. This encounter proved pivotal when the group met [[Bruce|Bruce]], a red-cloaked leader of the [[The Hoods]], who revealed that the "innkeeper’s" memory was actually a mental intrusion by "the hag": [[Maryln|Maryln]]. The session bridged deep lore and personal stakes, as the party discovered a journal detailing [[Ned Grindledark|Lord Grindledark]]'s horrific attempts to resurrect his son ([[Bruce|Bruce]]'s grandson) using "spiritcraft" and stitched body parts. Amidst these revelations, the party navigated a tense shopping trip where [[Theratina]]’s kleptomania strained her relationship with [[Callie Dawnsong|Cal]], and [[Roz Rozum|Roz]] discovered his shield, [[Jimmothy]], is sentient while he himself suffers "notches" of exhaustion from a cursed red diamond. A visit to the [[Blooming Remedy]] provided powerful magical boons through mystical petals and identified three mysterious vials, including one linked to the dread [[Lord Vecna]]. Ultimately, [[Bruce|Bruce]] offered the party a high-stakes contract: 1,000 gold pieces each to assassinate [[Maryln]] and secure her staff artifact for the pocket-portal book. The session concluded with the party weighing this dangerous alliance against the [[The Hoods]] as they prepare to hunt a shapeshifting enemy who can blink through reality.

### Political and Historical Context

[[Avenj]], [[Roz Rozum|Roz]], and [[Callie Dawnsong|Cal]] reflect on older history and what they know of the region.

They discuss a great war involving the Kingdom of Belrhodun (??). There were catastrophic losses in the war. As a result, the kingdom turned to a mercenary forces to ensure safety throughout the realm. One of the mercenary forces were called [[The Grindledarks]]. Grindledark Lords once hunted the [[20 Monsters]]. After those hunts were complete, and the monsters locked away, [[The Silent Fist]] rose up within the [[Campaigns/Under The Ash/Locations/Evermore/The Vel’Therin Expanse/index|Vel’Therin Expanse]] and have since taken sweeping control under martial-law-like conditions. So while [[Campaigns/Under The Ash/Locations/Evermore/The Vel’Therin Expanse/index|Vel'Therin]] has an army, [[The Silent Fist]] still holds great power within the city. 

[[Diamond of 20 Monsters|The Diamond of 20 Monsters]] was earlier described in the book as an artifact used by [[The Unknown]] to summon 20 monstrous creatures, only for them to be slain by the [[The Grindledarks|Grindledarks]].

As the group discusses, a scrap of paper hits [[Yharov Stonebrow|Yharov]]. It reads: “[[Theratina]] could use a new rapier.” In her room, [[Theratina]] is peaking out. [[Roz Rozum|Roz]] previously used [[locate-object-xphb|Locate Object]] on it while trying to track her after [[The Seer]] kidnapped her. But the party now realizes that she is no longer in possession of it. 

In addition to seeking out the man in the cloak from the vial, the party decides they will shop tomorrow and get [[Theratina]] a new rapier. 

The party decides to call it a night, and [[Raelor]], [[Avenj]], and [[Yharov Stonebrow|Yharov]] decide to take turns taking watch.

### The Grindledark Journal

The journal describes an obsessed writer who discovered the vessel, something tied to a master beyond the world.

He writes that all others failed:

- [[The Awakening]] shattered
- The Chain broke
- Armies became dust
- Old plans rotted

But he believes he has improved on all prior work by combining:

- Rituals
- Blood work
- Doctrine
- Flesh and spiritcraft

At first he thinks he must fill the center of the vessel.

Then he goes to [[Brackenmill]] and meets [[Payquyne]].

That location matters because [[Brackenmill]] was also where [[Aven Tide]] made her bargain with [[Maryln]] earlier in this same session, making the town feel less like a side location and more like a major knot where several of these storylines converge.

The writer falls in love with [[Payquyne]], forgets the work, and lives a human life. They have a son.

Then something monstrous kills her by taking her head, and leaves the son broken.

The writer decides to rebuild the child using powerful body parts:

- The arm of a butcher king
- The leg of a war champion
- The heart of a man executed three times who would not die
- The eyes of a laughing seer who foresaw his own death

He stitches the child together, calls to him, begs, commands, offers everything he is, and then his eyes open.

This journal provides the clearest emotional and procedural bridge yet between [[Ned Grindledark|Lord Grindledark]] - previously introduced as the [[drow]] creator of the [[Scary Frankenstein Guy]] and the larger body-horror/vessel project first glimpsed when the party fought the stitched [[The Awakening|Awakening]] agent in earlier sessions.
